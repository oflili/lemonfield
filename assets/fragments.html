The world of word

● Fragmented Lore Pack ● Environmental Narrative Assets ● Ritual & System Text Collection

Function:Rhythmic environmental narrative used for item descriptions, loading screen lore, or cursed inscriptions.

Design Goal:To create a "Linguistic Trap"—verses that sound sweet or nursery-rhyme-like but reveal deep corruption upon closer reading.

1. The Forbidden Plate

Resin on its skin / A coat from all sin Flowers grown inside limb / Yellow wasps crawl within Apple and fig, the forbidden sweet / Dressed as fruit, it lures the sick A plant that wilts, it bends and tilts / Plenty tricks, a plate of guilt

Context: Item description for "The Gilded Fruit" or an inscription found in a high-status dining hall.

Thematic Weight: Guilt, gluttony, and the decay hidden beneath high-society aesthetic.

2. The Watcher’s Litany

A world of innocence, all words born of lies Behold the redemption, was told by worn eyes The filth sitting on the ground / The rich giving it crown The illth stitching its wound / A lich coming as a sound Come one, my divine / Condone all its crimes Countdown, three two one / Bow down— An owl with nemesis A fowl is witnessing

Context: Chanted by NPCs in "sanity-Low" zones or hidden in fragmented diary entries.

Thematic Weight: Surveillance (The Owl/Fowl), social decay (Filth/Rich).

3. The Stage of the Rise

Gasp from the rage Gasp in the cage Engage since first page The plague climbs to stage Cloud the mass with shine false-bless Crowd amaze with finesse press Faze in their eyes. Glaze all the lies Graze all the haze. Craze all the faze Flash all the prize. Place all the price Phrase all the praise. Phase of the rise First day, free fair to play Mid day, stack them with rays Long day, let them stay Waylay, burn them to ash Blot and rot silent, sealed in a word-bound Rotten crowd silence still under the ground

Context: Found inscribed in an abandoned theater or a site of mass public "performance."

Thematic Weight: Text scrolls rapidly on the UI as the player approaches a "point of no return."

Impact: Increase guilt; Alters later dialogue; Permanently stains objects or language
