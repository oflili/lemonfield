[Core Service]

1. Narrative and Atmosphere Design

The architecture of how your game feels - where tension rises, where silence speaks, where the environment tells the story your dialogue can't.

➤ Atmospheric Integration Blueprint (10-20 page design document)

  • Audio direction & silence mapping
  • Environmental narrative placement strategy
  • Typography & visual tone guidelines
  • Tension escalation architecture
  • Implementation timeline with technical notes
➤ Emotional pacing architecture (scene-by-scene breakdown)
  • Psychological rhythm map for key moments
  • Player state progression tracking
  • Crescendo/valley placement for optimal impact
  • Integration with existing game systems
➤ Narrative Tension Mapping (visual diagram + written guide)
  • Where tension appears (silence, combat, ambient, BGM, environment)
  • How it builds, peaks, and releases
  • Player immersion triggers and psychological anchors
  • System-narrative synchronization points

2. Narrative Consultation (Concept + System Direction)

Direct access to strategic narrative thinking for your project. Not hand-holding - surgical precision on what's broken and how to fix it (format in consultation + follow-up document).

  • Core Premise Refinement - Is your narrative concept strong enough to carry the game? Does it have structural integrity?
  • Emotional Logic Mapping - Do your character motivations actually make psychological sense, or are they "video game logic"?
  • Player Experience Goals - What should players feel at each stage? Are your mechanics supporting or betraying that?

3. Interactive Structure

The systems that make your story playable - not cutscenes, but the mechanical expression of narrative (format in flowcharts + technical documentation + example scripting).

  • Dialogue Tree Architecture - Branching logic, emotional state tracking, consequence design
  • Choice Consequence Structure - What choices matter, how they ripple, when players feel the weight
  • Emotional State Tracking Logic - How the game "remembers" player actions and shifts tone accordingly
  • Variable-Based Narrative Flow - Technical implementation guide for dynamic storytelling

4. Lore & World Building

The sistematic fragments that give your world soul rather than explain it. Environmental storytelling that players piece together, not wiki entries (format in Google Doc with categorized content + style guide).

  • Environmental Text - What's written on walls, signs, objects that tells story through implication
  • Object Descriptions - Items that carry emotional weight and backstory without exposition dumps
  • Thematic Fragments - Lore pieces that establish tone and mystery without over-explaining
  • Tonal Consistency Guide - How to keep your world's voice unified across all writing

[Strategic Framework]

Deep-dive deconstruction of what's working and what's not - in your game or competitors'. The kind of analysis that appeared in my Love and Deepspace breakdown (10-20 page analysis document with actionable recommendations).

  • Strategic Analyses - Reverse-engineering how successful games manipulate player emotion (similar to the LDS audio analysis)
  • Systemic Design Papers - Does your current narrative design actually serve your goals? What's broken?
  • Emotional Retention Frameworks - How to keep players psychologically invested session-to-session
  • Design Risk Audits - What narrative/atmospheric choices might backfire? Where are you vulnerable?
