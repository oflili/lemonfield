1. The world of word

Fragmented Lore Pack | Environmental Narrative Assets | Ritual & System Text Collection

Function: A collection of rhythmic, atmospheric narrative fragments designed for diverse in-game uses such as item descriptions, loading screen lore, cursed inscriptions, and ambient storytelling.

Design Goal: To craft a "Linguistic Trap"—texts that initially feel familiar, simple, or even whimsical, like nursery rhymes or folk verses, but which subtly unravel into darker, more corrupt, and morally complex meanings upon closer inspection.

➤ Narrative State Test Pack → World-State Progression

Context: An evolving set of modular texts that reflect the moral and linguistic decay of the game world across its progression states.

Innocence State:

Words have been loved / It's the world of the folk The world is a trope / Words shine in gold

Trust State:

Words are given worth / The world, home of hope A world with a shop / Words make their road

Doubt State:

Words begin to doubt / The world is weaving its rope The world basks in smoke / All words morph into work

Corruption State:

The world wrote a note / All words are loathed Words have been bought / Its world is tied in a knot

Collapse State:

The world follow its plot / Words fill the slot Words of the sword / The world begins to rot

Thematic Weight: This text set signals shifts in the world’s state from innocence to collapse, to guide players emotionally through the narrative arc by shifting tone from hopeful and lyrical to dark and foreboding, reinforcing the player’s sense of changing stakes.

Implementation: Designed to be used in loading screens, ritual dialogue, and chapter transitions. By embedding these subtle linguistic cues, the text enhances player immersion and foreshadows world events without explicit exposition.

➤ Chant and Environmental Text → The Watcher’s Litany

Context: A ritualistic chant that appears in “sanity-low” zones or fragmented diaries, evoking themes of surveillance and social decay.

A world of innocence, all words born of lies Behold the redemption, was told by worn eyes The filth sitting on the ground / The rich giving it crown The illth stitching its wound / A lich coming as a sound Come one, my divine / Condone all its crimes Countdown, three two one / Bow down— An owl with nemesis A fowl is witnessing

Thematic Weight: This text deepens the atmosphere in corrupted or unstable areas of the game world, to create unease and reinforce the feeling of being watched and judged, while highlighting societal collapse.

Implementation: Spoken by NPCs or found as lore fragments, the chant’s repetition and symbolic language support environmental storytelling, subtly influencing player emotions and narrative understanding.

Context: This fragment depicts a society drawn into sp ectacle and manipulation, where initial excitement fades into deception, decay, and collapse—symbolizing the spread of a corrupting “plague” through performance and propaganda.

Glaze all the lies Graze all the haze. Craze all the faze Flash all the prize. Place all the price Phrase all the praise. Its rhythmic, layered language mirrors the theatrical and stage setting, underscoring the performative nature of public deception and downfall.

Implementation: Text scrolls rapidly on the UI as the player approaches a "point of no return", this is could heightens tension and foreshadows irreversible narrative shifts, deepening immersion through environmental storytelling.



2. Pulse and Whisper

Raw narrative materials for atmospheric horror and psychological games. These fragments can be integrated as environmental text, UI prompts, or NPC dialogue.

➤ Environmental Text Fragments

Function: Atmospheric prompts that build tension through implication.

  • "Your sin start having a pulse" - Guilt manifestation. Triggers after morally questionable choices.
  • "They sharpen their nails to dig the pit"- Accumulation of violence. Appears in areas of repeated conflict.
  • "All words is morph into world"- Meta-narrative. Reality shifts based on player dialogue.
  • "Eyes that could talk is a flaw" - Surveillance paranoia. Activates during extended observation.

➤ Interactive Object Concept

Function: Objects that reveal player nature through interaction.

  • The Pen of Truth - "Red for sin, blue for whim. Write something and see what's your color!" Moral barometer through color-coded writing. Player's language determines narrative path.
  • The Screaming Balloon - "Be careful, we don't want to hear it scream" Tests curiosity vs. consequence. Multiple interaction outcomes (carry, pop, abandon).
